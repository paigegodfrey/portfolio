About Me

I'm an analytical thinker with a passion for learning and desire to be challenged.

My background in finance has given me a unique perspective on problem solving and project management. After graduating from Cal Poly SLO in 2013, I spent 6 years working for Chevron in various corporate/manufacturing finance positions, entering through the company’s Finance Leadership Development Program. 

My experience with budget stewardship, business planning, and internal controls helped develop my business acumen, but I found myself most interested in building customer-facing tools and figuring out how to automate tasks. After months of soul-searching, attending meetups, and talking with folks in industry, I decided to pursue software engineering so I could spend my days using logic and creativity to solve complex problems. At the beginning of 2020, I enrolled at Rithm School — a 16-week, full-time program focused on React, Node, Python, PostgreSQL, and data structures.

After working on personal projects, interning at JumpOffCampus, and teaching computer science students at CodePath, I feel comfortable contributing to large codebases and working with a variety of tech stacks.

Please refer to my resume for additional details on my skills and experience.

Hobbies

When I'm not coding I like to stay physically active! I grew up doing Tae Kwon Do, earning my Second-Degree Black Belt before leaving for college. Moving up and down the West Coast with Chevron made it difficult to maintain a rank-based sport, so I've since taken up weightlifting and yoga.
